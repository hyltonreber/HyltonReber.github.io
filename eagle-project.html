Overview

The Project

Hope Lutheran Church & School — the elementary school I attended, and where I'd won several FIRST LEGO League robotics trophies — had no permanent way to display its students' competition history. Trophies and plaques were scattered around the building instead of telling one cohesive story.

For my Eagle Scout service project, I designed, built, and installed a wall-mounted display case to fix that — a permanent trophy case for the school's robotics, geography bee, and spelling bee trophies, with adjustable glass shelving sized to grow with future wins.

Design & Build

The case is a wood frame with sliding glass doors and adjustable glass shelves, stained dark walnut to match the existing hallway trim and plaques. It was originally planned to stand on the floor, but my project coach and I redesigned it mid-planning to mount flush against the wall instead — it fit the hallway better, and anchoring it to the wall meant it couldn't tip over.

Because of COVID-19 restrictions, we also moved the entire build off-site to my coach's home shop, where tools, space, and materials were already in place.

Leading the Project

I recruited and led 10 volunteers — fellow Scouts from Troop 310, adult leaders, and family — through planning, fundraising, and a multi-day build, raising the money almost entirely through congregation donations.

The hardest part wasn't the woodworking — it was finding enough meaningful work to keep every volunteer busy at once. The biggest lesson: leadership isn't about doing every task yourself, it's connecting the people who have the right skills to the work that needs to get done.
